Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Several signers of the Declaration of Independence are widely recognized for their historical significance, either for their role in the American Revolution or their later contributions to the United States. Here are some of the most famous:

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence. Later became the 3rd President of the United States. Known for articulating the core ideals of liberty and democracy.
John Adams
Advocate for independence and helped draft the Declaration. Later became the 2nd President of the United States. Known for his diplomacy and legal intellect.
Benjamin Franklin
Helped edit the Declaration. Renowned inventor, diplomat, and elder statesman. Later negotiated the Treaty of Paris that ended the Revolutionary War.
John Hancock
President of the Second Continental Congress. Famous for his large, bold signature on the Declaration so "King George could read it without his spectacles."
Samuel Adams
Leading figure in organizing resistance to British rule. Founded the Sons of Liberty. Cousin of John Adams.
Roger Sherman
Member of the "Committee of Five" that drafted the Declaration. Only person to sign all four major U.S. founding documents.
Robert Morris
Financier of the Revolution. Helped fund the war effort and later became Superintendent of Finance.

These individuals were central to the founding of the United States, both through their signatures and their wider contributions to independence and governance.
